How To Survive in Tsunami

Before a Tsunami
- Identify the signs of a tsunami such as earthquakes
loud roars from the ocean sudden rise or wall of water
and sudden draining of water that shows the ocean floor.
- Know and practice evacuation plans by mapping
the routes from your home.
- Work and play to a highland area pick shelters
that are at least a mile away from the coast.
- Strategize a family emergency communication plan,
sign up for the warning system in your community.
- Have earthquake and flood insurance policies through
the national food insurance program.

During a Tsunami
- If there is an emergency of a tsunami evacuate immediately
to a safe place and as high and as far island as possible.
- For those who are outside the tsunami zone stay in that
area unless told to move by the authorities.
- Grab something that floats when in the water.
- When on the boat face the direction of the water and head out to sea.

After a Tsunami
- Listen to a local alerts for information on areas
to avoid in places where shelters location are.
- Call or use social media or any mode of communication
available to communicate with family and friends.
- Avoid going near flood water as it might contain
debris or might be deeper than it seems.
- Do not touch any electrical equipment be alert for underground
or down power lines that can electrically charge water.
- Stay away from affected buildings and structures.
- Contact the health care provider for any injuries or sickness.
- Document property damage using pictures and conduct
inventory contact the insurance company for assistance.
