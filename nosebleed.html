How to Treat Nose Bleed

Nose bleed

A nosebleed (epistaxis) is bleeding from the nose.
The blood might go down the throat, causing nausea and vomiting.
Severe nosebleeds can involve both nostrils and,
in rare cases, lead to low blood pressure.

- Tell them to sit down, never lie down,
and keep the nose above the heart to help to control the bleeding.

- Get them to lean forward, never backwards,
this ensures that the blood drains out through the nose
rather than the back of the throat, which may block the airway.
- Ask the casualty to breathe through their mouth and pinch the soft part of the nose.

- You can give them a pad to catch any blood that may drain out.
After 10 minutes, release the pressure, reassess.
- If the bleed has not stopped, apply the pressure for a further ten minutes.
- If the bleeding is severe, or if you have not managed to control
the bleed after 30 minutes, it is time to call for an ambulance.
- Get them to sit down leaning forward, pinch the soft part of the nose,
and if necessary call for an ambulance.
