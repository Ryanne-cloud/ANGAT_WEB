What To Do If Objects Fall

Falling objects injure both children and adults.
While high storage is sometimes necessary, prevent accidents
by organizing items neatly, avoiding overfilled shelves, and storing heavy items low.
With young children, constant supervision of items on elevated surfaces is crucial.
Ideally, store nothing above shoulder height.
Always use safe methods (steps, ladders) to access high items.
While eliminating all risks is impossible, organization
and vigilance significantly reduce accident chances.

CAUSES:
- Unstable gait of toddler.
- Presence of objects on floor.
- Lack of supervision.
- Curiosity of the children

PREVENTION:
- Keep the floor free of toys and objects.
- Exercise close supervision when a toddler learns to walk.
- Never leave babies unattended on raised surfaces.
- Keep the floor dry.
- Ensure the bed rails of the baby cot are raised when the baby is in the cot.
- Always use a securely fitted safety harness in a pram,pushchair or high chair.
- Windows and doors must be locked to avoid misadventure by children.
- Avoid placing "step-stones" such as chair next to a window.
- Take extra care to avoid side-turning of chair.

FIRST AID
- Don't panic. Call for help if necessary.
- Check the level of consciousness of the infant/child.
- Examine the child if Airway is clear(e.g. can talk, cry or not);
If breathing is adequate and circulation is normal
(observe colour of face, depth and rate of breathing).
- If breathing and circulation is normal check for any other injuries of the body.
- If bleeding occurs, ensure there is no foreign body in the wound.
Apply direct pressure to stop bleeding by covering a clean gauze on it
and add pressure on the gauze by your hand. Elevate the injured limb.
