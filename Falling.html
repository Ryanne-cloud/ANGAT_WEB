What To Do When You Faint

Fainting, also called syncope, is a sudden,
brief loss of consciousness and muscle control that resolves on its own.
It's caused by reduced blood flow to the brain, usually due to low blood pressure.

- To help someone that is feeling faint and or dizzy
if you're able to go behind the person put one of your legs between that person's legs
to help carry their body weight making sure that their
head does not hit any objects and or does not fall onto the floor.

- What you want to do next is check for the person's responsiveness
ma'am can you hear me ma'am can you hear me if there
isn't any response you have someone call 9-1-1 right away.

- Also check for a pulse if there isn't a pulse you
want to start cpr if there is a pulse you can also

- Help the patient by lifting up their legs and elevating it.

Here's a tip if you happen to be by
yourself you're feeling faint and or dizzy.

- Find yourself a wall and try to gently lower yourself onto the floor
